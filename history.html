General History

Dundalk Town as so-named was founded in the 12th century by the Normans, however the area has been inhabited since at least 3700BC, with many of the present-day townlands predating the Norman conquest.

Sections


    • In its heyday of 1906, the railway branched off in four directions from Dundalk Station. One line headed east to Carlingford and Omeath, another west to counties Monaghan, Fermanagh, Tyrone, Donegal and Derry, and finally -- the surviving north and south lines to Belfast and Dublin respectively.
