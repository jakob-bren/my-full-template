General History

Dundalk Town as so-named was founded in the 12th century by the Normans, however the area has been inhabited since at least 3700BC, with many of the present-day townlands predating the Norman conquest.

    • In its heyday of 1906, the railway branched off in four directions from Dundalk Station. One line headed east to Carlingford and Omeath, another west to counties Monaghan, Fermanagh, Tyrone, Donegal and Derry, and finally -- the surviving north and south lines to Belfast and Dublin respectively.     • Up until the late 1960s, County Louth had a Gaeltacht region - one of the very last remnants of the East Ulster dialect. It was located in the foothills of the Cooley Mountains, above the village of Omeath, and was also bordered very closely by a similar Gaeltacht region in South Armagh. A documentary was made on the subject, which is available to listen to (here), as well as this, recording were made of native speakers recounting local folklore as well as basic conversation and grammar in the language - the numbers, days of the week. etc. They can be found here (doegen.ie)
